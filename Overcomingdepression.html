Coping with Depression: Tips for Overcoming Depression One Step at a Time

Depression drains your energy, hope, and drive, making it difficult to take the steps that will help you feel better. But while overcoming depression isn’t quick or easy, it’s far from impossible. You can’t just will yourself to “snap out of it,” but you do have more control than you realize—even if your depression is severe and stubbornly persistent. The key is to start small and build from there. Feeling better takes time, but you can get there by making positive choices for yourself each day.

How do you deal with depression?

Dealing with depression requires action, but taking action when you’re depressed can be hard. Sometimes, just thinking about things like exercising or spending time with friends can seem exhausting or impossible to put into action.

Tip 1: Reach out and stay connected

Getting support plays an essential role in overcoming depression. On your own, it can be difficult to maintain a healthy perspective and sustain the effort required to beat depression. Reach out for help even when it feels tough.

10 Tips for Staying Connected

  • Talk to one person about your feelings
  • Help someone else by volunteering
  • Have lunch or coffee with a friend
  • Ask a loved one to check in with you regularly
  • Accompany someone to a small get-together
  • Call or email an old friend
  • Go for a walk with a workout buddy
  • Schedule a weekly dinner date
  • Meet new people by joining a club
  • Confide in a teacher, coach, or clergy member

Tip 2: Do things that make you feel good

Push yourself to do things, even if you don’t feel like it. As you make time for fun activities, you’ll gradually feel more upbeat. Support your health, and develop a "wellness toolbox" to deal with depression:

  • Spend time in nature
  • Watch a funny movie or TV show
  • Talk to friends or family
  • Play with a pet
  • Take a long, hot bath
  • Do something spontaneous

Tip 3: Get moving

Exercise is a powerful depression fighter. Research shows regular exercise can be as effective as medication for relieving depression symptoms. Start small, even with a 10-minute walk to boost your mood.

Tip 4: Eat a healthy, depression-fighting diet

Your diet impacts your mood. Avoid sugar and refined carbs, and ensure you’re getting enough B vitamins and omega-3 fatty acids to help boost your mood.

Tip 5: Get a daily dose of sunlight

Sunlight can help boost serotonin levels and improve your mood. Spend at least 15 minutes a day outside, or use a light therapy box if you live in a low-sunlight area.

Tip 6: Challenge negative thinking

Depression puts a negative spin on everything, but you can challenge those thoughts. Ask yourself, “What’s the evidence this is true?” or “How would I view this situation if I weren’t depressed?”

When to seek professional help

If your depression gets worse despite self-help efforts, seek professional help. Therapy, medication, and lifestyle changes can make a difference.
